Here are some questions frequently asked by scholars

Can I use my statement of result as proof of achieving a CGPA above 3.5, given that my department doesn't provide access to final year results?

Generally, every scholar is paid with proof that he/she had a CGPA of 3.5 and above in that particular session. However, in these specific cases, you can provide us with your graduating CGPA and previous CGPAs and we can calculate the CGPA for the session.

What academic result can be used as a medical student who hasn't taken professional exams yet, considering only first-year results are available?

Medical school uses a pass and fail method and not GPAs. As long as you didn't fail or repeat a session. Provide us with that proof.

How can students from departments that only provide combined annual results upload separate semester results?

You can upload the CGPA twice. The calculation can also be uploaded.

For a 4-year or 5-year program, how many disbursements can a scholar expect to receive in total?

4 years receive 4 payments, 5 years receive 5 payments and 6 years, 6 payments.

How can graduating scholars upload their results when they still have 2 sessions payments outstanding?

Graduating students are always handled specially.

How do I know my scholarship award batch?

Your batch is based on the year you were awarded. If you were awarded in 2024, then your batch is 2023/2024.

In a case where my CGPA drops below 3.5, will I be dropped permanently?

The foundation will only pay you for sessions where your CGPA is 3.5 and above. Any session where you do not achieve that, you will not be paid.
